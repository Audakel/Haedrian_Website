We are Haedrian

We imagine a world where all people, no matter how remote, are connected through mobile technology; enabling them to create opportunity for themselves, as well as their families and community. We believe providing secure, simple, and affordable access to capital will achieve this.

About Haedrian

Every year, half a trillion dollars changes hands around the world in the form of remittances; often from citizens of developing nations who work in other countries but send the money back to family. Companies like Western Union and MoneyGram are taking an average of 9% to 25% of that money in fees to support a bloated and antiquated business model.

Additionaly, nearly 3 billion adults do not have access to a bank to help them use their assests effectively; traditional banks simply cannot afford to serve small transactions and remote locations.

There is hope for these people. Companies pioneering mobile banking have found it profitable to serve poor in developing nations because many of these people have mobile phones, and the marginal cost of processing a digital transactions is near zero.

Inexpensive android phones are proliferating in developing nations, the price of Bitcoin is stabilizing and gathering enormous traction in many parts of the developing world. Using android phones paired with the Bitcoin protocol creates a financial system for the developing nations that is levels of magnitude more efficient, cheaper, and secure than current systems.

Now is the time to connect 3 billion people to the global economy and free them to transform their lives.

  • No more paper forms or going into stores to transfer funds
  • No more huge transfer or currency exchange fees
  • No more waiting days for the transfer
  • No more storing money under your bed or in livestock
  • No more being cut off from the global economy

Mobile Phone Growth

Why choose to launch on Android? It is ubiquitous, with 82.7% world mobile market share and with “smart” phones already available for less than $40 at retail.

Ericsson Mobility estimates that in the next 3-4 years there will be nearly 5.6 billion smartphone subscriptions in the world, with most growth in new purchases fueled from developing nations.

Connectivity Growth

Smartphones are definitely becoming cheaper, but that’s not the sole reason for their global spread; data plans are becoming far more inexpensive. Dozens of companies such as Facebook, Virgin Galatic, oneWeb have seen the potential growth a connected world would offer and are trying to provide internet access to less developed areas of the globe.

Much attention has been focused on Elon Musk’s SpaceX raising $1 billion in funding from Google and Fidelity to fund development and launch of 700 extremely low orbit mini-satellites which will eventually give very low cost/ high-speed Internet access to the much of the world.

Only about 40% of the world, or 3.05 billion people have access to the internet. What does the world look like when that number doubles?

Bitcoin Protocol

What is Bitcoin? It's a distributed peer-to-peer, highly cryptographical, digital currency; it's like electronic cash you can use to pay friends or merchants, but levels of magnitude more efficient, cheaper, and secure than current systems. The protocol assures that not only is every single transaction that ever takes place, recorded, but also verified by all users.
