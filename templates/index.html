We imagine a world where all people, no matter how remote, are connected through mobile technology; enabling them to create opportunity for themselves, as well as their families and community. We believe providing secure, simple, and affordable access to capital will achieve this.

About Haedrian

Every year, half a trillion dollars changes hands around the world in the form of remittances; often from citizens of developing nations who work in other countries but send the money back to family. Companies like Western Union and MoneyGram are taking anywhere from 10%-25% of that money in fees to support a bloated and antiquated business model.

Additionaly, nearly 3 billion adults do not have access to a bank to help them use their assests effectively; traditional banks simply cannot afford to serve small transactions and remote locations.

There is hope for these people. Companies pioneering mobile banking have found it profitable to serve poor in developing nations because many of these people have mobile phones, and the marginal cost of processing a digital transactions is near zero.

Inexpensive android phones are proliferating in developing nations, the price of bitCoin is stabilizing and gathering enormous traction in many parts of the developing world. Using android phones paired with the bitCoin protocol creates a financial system for the developing nations that is levels of magnitude more efficient, cheaper, and secure than current systems.

Now is the time to connect 3 billion people to the global economy and free them to transform their lives.

  • No more paper forms or going into stores to transfer funds
  • No more huge transfer or currency exchange fees
  • No more waiting days for the transfer
  • No more storing money under your bed or in livestock
  • No more being cut off from the global economy

How does it work?

Lets take the example of Juan who is in the US and wants to send money to his family in Mexico. He would connect with Coinbase and issue a buy order for the amount of bitCoins he wanted and pay a 1% fee to convert the currencies. Sending and receiving bitCoin is free so the transfer to his families wallet would just cost him the time it takes to press a send button. Juan's family would then use a Mexican bitCoin exchanges, such as mexBt to exchange the bitCoins for pesos and send it to their bank account, or if they don't have an account, simply pick it up from an ATM. Since the fee is only .5% you can imagine how happy Juan will be with the new found savings from not paying +10% fees with Western Union or MoneyGram.

What about the inverse of that story? Lets say Juan's family doesn't have a bank account but wants to send money to him in the US. They would deposit pesos or dollars in one of the 130,000 stores that have partnered with mexBt to load their bitCoin wallet, and send to Juan. They can even start saving in bitCoins if they would like; if they are worried about volatility they could buy a price lock which mexBt offers to guarantee future value.

How about those who have no access to any sort of a "traditional" bank? Kenya, home of M-Pesa, is a perfect location as 74% of Kenyans "would feel comfortable investing money in virtual currency, but few have access to "traditional" banks." Since the majority of commerce in Kenya is run through mobile phones, bitCoin would enable them to not only participate in local commerce, but global commerce. If they choose not to use M-Pesa, they download the app, create a bank account, and start transacting with the 1,000's of merchants who already accept bitCoin. They could also connect to Kipochi to exchange bitCoins to M-Pesa.

bitCoin Protocol

What is bitCoin? It's a distributed peer-to-peer, highly cryptographical, digital currency; it's like electronic cash you can use to pay friends or merchants, but levels of magnitude more efficient, cheaper, and secure than current systems. The protocol assures that not only is every single transaction that ever takes place, recorded, but also verified by all users.

Bitfinex

Bitfinex

Bitfinex is a platform providing advanced trading tools like margin trading, short selling and liquidity swaps to buy or sell bitCoins, Litecoins and Darkcoins. Bitfinex was launched in 2013 and has grown in a short span to be one of the largest exchanges going by the trading volume (USD).

The platform is currently in a beta testing phase and is looking to add new and advanced features going forward.

Bitso

A Mexican company that trades bitCoin with Mexican pesos, Bitso offers several funding and withdrawal options, such as SPEI, Bank Wire, and more.

Additionally, you can finance your Bitso account in person at more than 130,000 locations nationwide, including Oxxo, Extra, 7/11, Chedraui, Elektra, and Wallmart, among others.

Bitstamp

Bitstamp

Bitstamp, a Slovenia based bitCoin exchange, is one of top dogs in terms of trading volume. It facilitates instant buy-sell orders for USD/BTC pair with withdrawals and deposits available in currencies other than dollars as well.

The exchange offers trading using limit orders where a pre-determined price can be set for buy and sell orders. Bitstamp has earned a strong reputation in the bitCoin world.

There is a fee levied on withdrawals - the fee for SEPA is fixed at 0.90 after conversion into Euros while the fee for any international withdrawal is 0.09% (minimum fee being $15).

Bitsource.org

Bitsource.org

Bitsource.org by bitCoin Source is one of the newer platforms for trading. The exchange launched in 2014 and is based in Belize. One of the features that enhance its appeal is the acceptance of deposits by various methods including credit card payments like Visa, Discover Card, MasterCard, and American Express in addition to methods like ACH transfer.

A fee of 1% is charged per trading transaction. There are additional charges depending on the deposit option used. In case of credit cards, an interchange rate of 2.99% plus 0.30 applicable while for an eCheck/ACH transfer, a fee of $0.25 per transaction is deducted.

BTCChina

BTCChina

BTCChina is among the largest platforms for buying and selling bitCoins based on trading volumes. The exchange was founded in 2011 and is one of the oldest exchanges as well. It supports trading of bitCoins and Litecoins done in the Chinese currency (CNY); however, it allows for deposits and withdrawals in Hong Kong and U.S. dollars also.

The liquidity offered by the exchange due to its high volumes is one of its advantages. It also offers secure storage of bitCoins (offline) as the exchange uses many cold storage techniques for the purpose.

The exchange does not charge any deposit and trading fee for BTC, LTC and CNY; however, it does charge a withdrawal fee.

CampBX

CampBX

CampBX is a United States-based bitCoin exchange with a number of advanced trading features. Some of these features include Spend-X trades, short selling, and margin trading, although the latter two are not yet active.

CampBX promises 99.99% uptime and is audited daily by security auditor McAfee. CampBX also has Google TOTP two-factor authentication.

CampBX only accepts USD at the moment, but they are working on accepting EUR. Fees for both quick and advanced orders are 0.55%, with bulk discounts available. CampBX also has its own Testnet version, which lets users practice trading with easily-obtained coins akin to play money.

Coinbase

Coinbase

Coinbase has emerged as one of the high profile names in the bitCoin world, placing itself on the top as a one stop solution. It’s a wallet for storing, spending, buying and accepting bitCoins, acts as a bitCoin processor (merchant tool) for many merchants and businesses (example; Expedia, Overstock.com, Dell), and is among the top bitCoin exchanges.

Coinbase is a San Francisco, California based company. While its initial offering was primarily restricted to the U.S., it has entered Europe and provides services to around 18 countries.

There is a fee for each transfer from dollars to bitCoin or vice versa, charged at 1%, plus a $0.15 bank fee.

Cryptsy

Cryptsy

Cryptsy is a platform not only for bitCoins, but also the variants which are collectively called altcoins. The exchange provides services to trade more than 200 different types of cryptocurrencies currently, including the names like Litecoin, Dogecoin, MazaCoin, Peercoin and so on.

The exchange is headquartered in Florida and is a FinCEN registered MSB (Money Services Business). Cryptsy plans to offer services to merchants who are open to accepting the different digital currencies as a mode of payment.

The trading fee is 0.20% for buyers and 0.30% for sellers, while the transaction fee varies with the coin trading in.

Ice3x

Ice3x

Ice3x is a bitCoin exchange from Africa. It positions itself as an easy-to-use exchange, and doesn’t have any advanced trading or exchange features. Security-wise, Ice3x stores its bitCoins in encrypted offline wallets, which are then stored in secure bank vaults. Ice3x also provides two-factor authentication.

Ice3x supports USD, Zar Rand and Nigerian Naira.

Kraken

Kraken

Kraken is a feature loaded trading platform with fast deposits and withdrawals for professional and advanced traders. The exchange caters well to the needs of more sophisticated traders, but that doesn’t turn it unsuitable for beginners.

The digital currency options available at Kraken are bitCoin, Litecoin, Dogecoin, Namecoin, Ripple and Ven. The exchange supports Euro, U.S. Dollar and South Korean Won.

The trade fee is dependent on two factors; one is the currency pair, the other is the 30 day trading volume. In addition, certain charges need to be paid for deposits and withdrawals which are over and above the trading fee.
